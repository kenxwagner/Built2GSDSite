Here is another study that will show you the excellent job the Benton County Assessor’s office is doing.  Go to:   Washington Department of Revenue: Property Tax Statistics

Once there click on Annual Ratio Study Results. You will see Benton County has a Real Property 2009 ratio of 94.2% and a Personal Property ratio of 95.3%.  These percentages of ratio are determined by Department of Revenue and with these excellent Ratio Percentages that are better than all the Counties that border Benton County this saves us money.  You may wonder how this can save us on taxes.  That’s because the lower this “ratio rate” is the higher a County must pay towards the State Tax.  In other words the portion of property taxes going to the State Government has an adjusted levy rate that goes up (increases) as your Ratio gets further away from 100%.

The Benton County Assessor’s office is one of the top Assessor’s offices in the State. When it comes to measuring the uniformity and level of assessments, only one other County, besides Benton County has achieved a top-status rating for the last five years in a row.

Every year the Washington State Office of Program Research publishes the results titled “Measuring Real Property Appraisal Performance in Washington’s Property Tax System”. This Research group uses the International Association of Assessing Officers (IAAO) standards for performance, to measure the level and uniformity assessments of all Counties in Washington State.

There are eight assessment categories used to measure uniformity and level of assessment. For the last five years only two Counties, Benton and Clark have maintained the prestigious honor of 100% rating year after year.

Taxpayers can feel assured with this excellent measure of standards in Benton County, that property taxes are distributed equally in proportion to assessed value. Counties that do not meet this top standard have a lower degree of uniformity, thus creating an inequity, which brings about some property owners paying more than their appropriate share of property taxes while other owners pay less than their share in that same county.

Appreciation and thanks go to the professional accredited appraisers that have maintained this excellent rating.  We will do our best to maintain this A+ rating in Benton County.
